Systematic Awesome

The Manifesto of Systematic Awesome

If we’re going to casually help people who ask us for things, we might as well respect that spirit and really do so like we mean it -- use our brainpower to use that same amount of time to be actually effectively helpful. Maybe I can’t help someone directly myself, but there’s someone in the world who can. If I really care about helping, then maybe I could help them FIND that person.

The mission of “systematic awesome” is much like the mission of deontological ethics – do nothing except that it could become a universal law “I ought never to act except in such a way that I could also will that my maxim should become a universal law” (G 4:402) ~Kant . Next time you’re moved to help someone who asks you for something, I invite you to be consistent with this impulse and see how it feels to take a step towards helping everyone. And also to help people who don’t ask or can’t ask but actually need the help. To actually care!

Goals

Include everyone in the circle of human care – create a digital advocate for everyone’s needs, an issue tracker for society

Create tools that help us connect the dots of our thoughts, connect our open gestalts, so that we can strike at the roots of problems instead of the branches

Align intellectual soulmates

Start open source movement for all knowledge – the “global triple store”

The global knowledge tree; homepage on reddit daily so you get caught up in situational vs universal news

Build the codex of human know-how

Create the global strategic idea reserve 

Lift the fog of nonconnection that sits on the world. Enable the global mind to meditate.

2025-12-29

My favorite question to ask engineers is "what makes software hard?" A team of thousands of employees keeps google docs running yet a single talented, motivated engineer can re-make 10% of google docs in a month. Why is it so hard for Google? Ultimately—software has very few hard *technical* problems left. The largest issue we face now is one of *coordination*. We are *awful* at coordinating what software gets built, *awful* at communicating how that software works once it is, and *awful* at composing that software into our larger digital landscape.

We've solved this coordination problem in other domains before - we don't need to coordinate our economies - money acts as our tool for coordination. We don't need to coordinate our information - language acts as our tool for coordination. But 'Money' and 'Language' are terrible tools for coordinating computers.

The tension in this missing coordination has been building up for decades. The pressure is at a breaking point, pulled even tighter by the force of AI and LLMs - it's begging to be solved.

So let's solve it.

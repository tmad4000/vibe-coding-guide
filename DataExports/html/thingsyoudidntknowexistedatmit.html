Athletics/Outdoor/Physical:

Snow!

The Stata amphitheatre steps can be made into a giant sled track with enough snow!

You can rent skate-style cross-country skis at the Weston Ski Track off the green line -- they’re open all winter!

Trick to building snow shelter: make big, packed mound of snow, stick sticks in 8in deep, then tunnel in and hollow it out from inside until you reach sticks

Trick for warming hands: put arms by sides, straighten elbows, flex wrists, and move shoulders up and down. You will feel dramatic pumping sensation in hands

Free tai chi/qigong classes at MIT! One class, run by I think the MIT Traditional Medicine Society, Ashdown Tai Chi Workshop is 3:30-5:00pm on Sundays during term in the Hulsizer Room in Ashdown. Email tms-exec@mit.edu or cyx@mit.edu.

Professors:

MIT Faculty-Student Dinners - groups of 3 to 6 undergraduates can invite a faculty member to dinner and the UA will reimburse you. (Spring 2013 Signup Link)

Prof. Junot Diaz - Literature/CMS professor, Pulitzer prize winner, MacArthur fellow, amazing teacher

Prof. Joe Haldeman - Hugo and Nubula award winner, teaches Sci-fi writing. It’s amazing to watch his brain spin stories!

Prof. Noam Chomsky[b] - Father of Linguistics. Political activist

Prof. Seymour Papert - Cognitive Psychologist, author of Mindstorms

Prof. Marvin Minsky - Father of AI. Teaches class Society of Mind! - his lectures can be like literature -- he manages to tie them up with just the right words, e.g. ‘joke” society of mind 2013 2nd or 3rd lecture

Prof. Walter Lewin - legendary teacher, occasionally still lectures

Prof. John Harbison - most famous living American composer, winner of Pulitzer prize in music and McArthur Genius grant

Restaurants

Nearby “Every Day” Restaurants:

Thelonious Monkfish -- the best, wildest avant-garde sushi you've ever had -- I like the Red Riding Hood Roll, which includes strawberries. Also has excellent regular sushi, nonsushi asian fusion, and vegetarian options; bacon pad-thai

Moqueca -- an AWESOME Brazillian restaurant. Inman Sq (East of central)

Life Alive -- a wonderful, beautiful, hippy-friendly organic foodsy restaurant that makes me :D. In Central

Clover -- another wonderfully tasty health-foody restaurant in Harvard/Inman squares. Also my favorite food truck in Kendall. 100% vegetarian

Asmara -- nice eritrean (ESSENTIALLY ETHIOPIAN) place. Comment: -1 from ethiopian friend

Flour -- phenomenal breakfast/lunch food. Just on Mass Ave towards Random Hall. Pretty expensive

Om Restaurant -- (Harvard Sq)

Fire and Ice -- a terrific Mongolian BBQ (Harvard Sq and Back Bay)

O Sullivan’s -- AWESOME burgers. about $10 but definitely worth it. On beacon street.

Beantown Taqueria -- a lot of very good Mexican food for reasonable prices on Mass Ave

Technique

The Breakfast Club -- Allston. Phenomenal, affordable breakfast and lunch not too far from Pika! Excellent non-bitter coffee!

The Friendly Toast -- awesome breakfast-all-day restaurant in Tech square, best described as being similar to “a warm hug”

Sebastians -- best salads/sandwiches/lunch crepes in Kendall

Brookline Lunch (in Central Sq) - $6 steak and eggs. mound of awesome veggies and homefries

                Emma’s Pizza -- wacky and normal pizzas made to order (Potato, cranberry, bacon?)

                Ole -- mexican breakfast in inman sq. cactus.

                Sichuan Garden -- (Brookline Village) best Sichuanese food in the state; really authentic, delicious, and dangerously spicy; easily accessible via Green Line; ask for the Chinese-only menu if they don’t give it to you (has English descriptions on it now-a-days)

                Mary Chung’s -- real chinese food. make sure to ask for Alice. she’ll tell you what to order on the menu. Over 20 years old and alums love the “suan la chow sho”

                Moody’s Falafel palace -- open late. incredible wraps and plates. super cheap. only takes cash.

                Pepper Sky -- awesome. awesome. thai food. way better than thailand cafe.

                Cambridge Brewing Co. -- (Kendall) interesting in-house brews and well-done locally sourced bar food

                InsomniaCookies.com -- near Harvard, but delivers

                Kika Tapas.com -- Kendall Square

                Veggie Galaxy - 100% vegetarian, super hipster, down Mass Ave a block past Tosci’s

Nearby “Special Occasion” Restaurants (in order from least to most expensive):

                Green St. -- (Central) awesome american food and great bar, classy hole in the wall

Rendezvous -- (Central) Great Northern African-contemporary fusion for a reasonable price; prix fixe and Monday night tapas at the bar

                Blue Room -- (Kendall) run by the same people as Central Bottle; fusion cuisine with a focus on grilled; Sunday all-you-can-eat brunch buffet is incredible

                West Bridge -- (Kendall) affordable, exciting contemporary cuisine (ask for the bread); amazing mixed drinks

                Oleana -- (Kendall) astonishingly good Middle Eastern; must book well in advance

                Bondir -- (Kendall) locally sourced collages of ingredients you’ve never dreamed of; ultimate date night; book well in advance

                Salts -- (Central) high French cuisine; super romantic

                Cuchi Cuchi (Central) Around-the-world small plates, very charming prohibition-era decorations and great (non-)alcoholic drinks

Craigie on Main -- (Central) fantastic contemporary American cuisine, though on the pricy side; amazing bar for mixed drinks, head bartender is Jared Sadoian ‘09; best burger in Massachusetts

                L’Espalier -- (Prudential) perhaps the best restaurant in Boston; expensive, but aim for the more affordable Wine Mondays: $65 for 4 course prix fixe + wine pairing

                O Ya -- (Financial District) astonishing sushi and Japanese small plates; very expensive but will be one of the most memorable meals of your life

Purpose of Document
Post-scarcity means something different to every person who utters the words. It will be helpful to define it a little bit more. For that reason I will bring in the first paragraph of wikipedia on Post-scarcity Economy. 
Post-scarcity is a theoretical economic situation in which most goods can be produced in great abundance with minimal human labor needed, so that they become available to all very cheaply or even freely. Post-scarcity does not mean that scarcity has been eliminated for all goods and services, but that all people can easily have their basic survival needs met along with some significant proportion of their desires for goods and services. Writers on the topic often emphasize that some commodities will remain scarce in a post-scarcity society. 
Even with this definition there are many types of post scarce societies. In some systems, its people may still be under the rule of an authoritarian regime. There are futures where post scarce-societies are quite dystopian. Too many to list. To hone in on what is desired will take its own document with many differing perspectives. 
Let’s for now assume that all conscious beings desire autonomy and we desire a system where everyone is given the tools for themself to be self-sufficient with little to no work. Freeing virtually all time to their own pursuits. 
While there are many things that are needed to support a free, open, and wonderful future we will need to focus our efforts on specific areas because of the scope of the project. The areas we will focus are:
* Access to energy - Without energy the engine of humanity seizes. 
* Access to water
* Access to food
* Access to shelter and a place to call home. (Land and autonomy)
* Access to healthcare

Healthcare
* Post Disaster (Emergency Medicine)
   * vast majority of modern healthcare costs
   * Majority of incidents traffic accident related (change transportation, change medicine)
* Time to treatment is a major factor in survivability
* Open Source Medical Equipment: 
   * Many of the supplies and devices used in medicine are very disconnected in price from manufacturability, and are much more a reflection of the corporate/political/legal/insurance landscape.  Since COVID, many groups are building viable open source (free) alternatives to the proprietary designs.  We saw this with facemasks and ventilators, in particular - proving even complex stuff can be replicated (or improved)
   * Major hurdles include an approval process (FDA, CE in Europe) poorly geared for treating designs independent of companies or manufacturers, or approval processes that take millions of dollars (in fees) and many months of delay - fairly inaccessible to small decentralized actors.  Missing some kind of middle-organization certifying designs with open source testing and verification before pairing with manufacturers and serving designs to FDA etc on a silver platter. (See link for groups working at this)
   * (Alternatively, create new verification agencies/models from scratch in developing world or native lands)
   * Links:

Demand and Story
  



Much of the problem is people not realizing what’s possible, and financiers willing to push projects that don’t have a public demand.  But the public doesn’t necessarily know what it wants - it needs to be inspired first
We largely rely on old sci-fi and fictional media.  Many of the engineers in Clubhouse were inspired by early fiction to inspire their career paths (and those are just the few who made it).
* We need to establish new demand for new paths, using inspiring stories and new media.
      * We need inspiring visions of the future 
      * Slow Media
* Are there existing stories close to what we’re aiming for? Is there a way to bring the public around faster than inspiring the next generation?
   * Most visions of the future are technology focused and many are dystopian. We need biocentric and green visions, positive visions, visions of different types of ownership and governance, low tech / indigenous inspired visions… etc 
   * Are there advertisers or financiers with experience pushing very new “products” (or societal directions)?
      * Laurel: marketing and design
      * Peter Thiel?
* Demand makes politics and financing MUCH easier.  Especially relevant for controversial technologies (e.g. nuclear)
* Another part of the problem is that people are gun-shy to actually make change in the world, used to things being very complex and relegated to experts.  Normalizing innovation in the logistics space might help there:
   * Gamification of “Work”:
      * E.g. Satisfactory, Factorio, Factory I/O, Space Camp, Minecraft
* Examples needing demand:
   * Maggot farming!  Public is squeamish, but the waste => protein efficiency is crazy good, and flavor better than you’d expect

Governance
* Self-governance structure of project(s)
* Microdemocracy:
   * https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2016/12/microdemocracy-is-a-high-tech-solution-to-americas-current-system/
* Plans should try to be agnostic to society flavor (capitalism/communism mix) and aim to integrate with the here and now (for near-term projects at least)
* Future evolution of projects should (needs to?) carefully consider ownership and ensure the community (local or global, depending on project) has equal, fair ownership and say on the future direction.  This is particularly important for essential services vital to survival - where the wrong person/group with leverage can threaten everyone
* Security of voting systems/ownership structures/software/etc is a big issue and only recently are people confident this can be done online at all (crypto/blockchain voting - new and shaky).  Rule of thumb: Anything connected to the internet (at least)  can be hacked, and any person in charge can be corrupted.  
   * Note: If you care less about privacy and are willing to have public identities voting/governing that’s much easier to secure.  But - also easier to bribe people! (private voting = can lie to briber while still taking the money)
* Keywords: Open Source, community ownership, decentralized governance, crypto/blockchain verified identities, decentralized delegative voting, UBI
* Related Issues: Founder syndrome, heavy-handed investor control, founders selling out, ego issues, corporate buyouts, government shutdown 
* Logistics: Live organization of this group itself (mostly volunteer)
   * Clubhouse: timezone-distributed leaders
   * MMO Guild leader style
* Inclusiveness:
   * Make sure to design with the most marginalized/underrepresented in mind to ensure not just imposing
   * e.g. possibly ensure “product owner”/consultant equivalent is african woman (e.g. Sarah Emerson’s suggestion)

Politics
* Often the biggest hurdle to big projects :)
* Expect to optimize for very different factors.  Job creation, popularity, benefits to insiders (some might call bribery), nearsightedness to just political term lengths (funding long term projects very tough)
* Get used to possibility (necessity?) of delegating public image of project to professionals who know how to popularize it
   * Politicians love this role, especially if they are sheltered from any risk or downsides with someone else to blame!
* Beware that the majority of these solutions directly butt heads with existing industries (oil, car, farming, real estate, etc) with deep benches of lobbyists, politicians and extra-legal problem solvers with every incentive to obstruct.  A pessimistic (but realistic) view of history reveals this is a big reason the way the world is - and if anything, we are looking for exceptions
   * On that note, this is a special time in history, where the way things are is in serious upheaval, and the established powers may be vulnerable to disruption

Definitions of Post-Scarcity:

(Warren Koch) Attempting to map a few characterizations, in loose ascending order of difficulty to achieve and/or urgency.  Feel free to modify or augment, especially if you have a particular target in mind.  Meant to be a characterization of the different target levels, rather than comprehensive definitions.
If someone has a handy list of all the various goals/definitions of post-scarcity out there, please link!


“Human Survival Post-Scarcity” / “Basic Needs Post-Scarcity”

* Living Standards:
   * clean water
   * nutritious food (though not necessarily the most nutritious, or most tasty)
   * Adequate shelter from typical environments
   * Reasonable medical coverage of “treatable” diseases
      * Does not necessarily include aging-based diseases (cancers, heart disease, DNA deterioration)
      * As a baseline, should aim to include typical medical access of a 21st century first world middle class citizen (reminder, this is the least ambitious post scarcity definition)
   *    * Aims to make these guarantees in both typical environments and with measures in place for emergency conditions (fires, floods, pandemics, etc)
   * Essentially: reasonable freedom from survival and economic pressures to maintain a reasonable human living standard
* Stable System
   * This system must meet the above goals without destroying nature’s (or the system augmenting nature’s) ability to perpetuate itself
   * Use of non-renewable resources must have distant deadlines, with reasonable plans for replacing those resources (e.g. asteroid mining for gold might be a reasonable assumption a hundred years from now.  Use of oil without accounting for the cost of its use and replacement is not )
   * Since the current system is not currently stable in regards to Climate Change, solving current logistics and resource problems without perpetuating runaway environmental effects is a major challenge here, cleaning up after the old system
* Labor:
   * These basic needs can be met with very little labor or economic cost
      * Rule of thumb standard: max 1 month of labor per year (?) (9-5 working hours)
   * No particular assumption on when that labor might be needed (e.g. lots of work around harvest time might still be post-scarcity)
   * May include other labor imposed by community/state etc, but not necessary for living standards (i.e. political-based state)
* Notable things this definition of Post-Scarcity would NOT encompass or have an opinion on:
   * Human Rights:
      * Has no opinion or guarantee on the vast majority of Human Rights
         * These are to be guaranteed by the political reality of the citizen
         * https://opinionfront.com/list-of-human-rights
      * However, using technological advances, expands on the “Right to Rest”, requiring far fewer working hours to guarantee “Right to Adequate Living Standard”
         * May include other labor imposed by community, but not necessary for living standards
   * Political Landscape:
      * Indifferent to capitalist/communist slant of governance, as long as above conditions are met
         * E.g. Despite basic needs met, there may be wide wealth inequality
      * Makes no guarantees on:
         * Safety/threats 
            * from fellow human beings (crime)
            * from one’s own government/state/community
            * from external governments/states/communities
            * from enlistment in army/militia of government/state/community
         * Privacy and ownership of data standards
         * Access to economic prosperity and ability to “climb the ladder” beyond basic needs met
         * Access to community, psychological well-being, family (to the extent these are politically-imposed, not naturally/economically)
         * Access to travel (may have commitments or restrictions)
         * Access to stable living conditions (may need to relocate sometimes.  Can count this similar to labor below)
         * Access to uncensored information and education
         * Other human rights unrelated to nature or biological causes



“Human Thriving Post-Scarcity”
* TODO - feel free to split this up or redefine
* Essentially, the same as above but higher standards.
   * Best medical treatment for everyone (with some small acceptable propagation lag time from state of the art)
   * Eat basically anything you want
   * Live basically anywhere
   * Mansions for all
   * Travel, transport for all
   * Freedom of information etc (perhaps more optimistic, or technologically ambitious, than above on guaranteeing political rights)
   * Fully-Automated Gay Space Luxury
   * Access to reasonable economic opportunity (if an “economy” even still exists)
   * Access to the latest society inventions and ideas (with some small acceptable propagation lag time from state of the art)


“Biosphere Post-Scarcity (Pre-Industrial Levels)”
* TODO rename or merge, just adding this as distinct consideration or add-on
* Human survival post scarcity likely a prerequisite to avoid desperate behavior
* Dedicated Areas:
   * Nature is allowed space and freedom to grow unmolested by humans in designated sanctuaries
      * These may be huge
   * Aim is to preserve state of nature for research of pre-human influence
* Re-Wilding:
   * Nature is actively augmented with human technology to increase its growth and elevate biodiversity
   * Humans may supply additional food sources, resources, or light sources for plants
   * Nature is cultivated intelligently for sustainability targets
* Genetic Restoration
   * Use of DNA banks and/or gene sequencing to restore genetic biodiversity and re-integrate endangered species
* Target is to reach state of nature before industrial human civilization mass extinctions / collapse, or as close as we can...

“Biosphere Post-Scarcity (Pre-Human)”
* Same as above, but with woolly mammoths ;)


“Biosphere Post-Scarcity (Post-Nature)”
* We use human inventions to “elevate” (quotes used due to inherent controversy) species to similar post-scarcity conditions to human standards
   * Feed every animal (and insect), as much as it wants
* Optional: “elevate” species to consciousness… through ideal conditions + breeding or gene sequencing (again, controversial)

“Energy Post-Scarcity”
“Karashev Type I” https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kardashev_scale
* Might be pre-requisite to some of above, but distinct concept
* Enough energy that a lot of human-level problems become trivial, at least for populations achievable in the next century
* Fusion power or equivalent scale

 #Book your brain is a time machine
<>Aleks ivanov
What a wonderful book this is! Highly recommend to anyone interested in the perception of time and consciousness.

 A popular myth is that because of the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics, it levaes room for free will. However, even though QM is probabilistic it is still nonetheless deterministic. If you are a ball falling along a track to you it's all the same whether it is a single lane track or a track with a bifurcation
(n-furcation) with some probability of continuing on a given path. Nothing depends on you. Maybe free will is just a feeling, a mental construct conjured by the brain to make sense of our choices.

Equally interesting is the perception of time. In physics there is space-time in general
relativity where time becomes interdependent of space. In QM however time has a different meaning where it is necessary to describe the evolution of the system. As far as I got it many laws and physical systems are reversible and there is an inherent
symmetry going forwards or backwards. However, in our everyday conscious experience we do have a feeling of flow and the "arrow of time". To make sense of things, statistically speaking, entropy explains why many things seem a one way thing, boiling egg, breaking a glass in many pieces and why there is some directionality. Even if we assume some sort of directionality, according to physics there is nothing special about the present. Past, present and future may exist at the same time in a "block universe". Different observers, according to their speed/state can observe the same events in different order. Laws of GR don't ban time travel. So if there is nothing special about the present how come it feels so special to us? What gives us the perception of time flowing? Is time really flowing in the physical universe or it is just an illusion made by the brain? If it's not a real phsysical thing, does it  provide an evolutionary advantage. Fascinating stuff.

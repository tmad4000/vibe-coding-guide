Accounting
Most popular options are:
* Pilot – haven’t used before but overall heard good review from founders (but most founders haven’t tried others)
* Indinero – had to spend time to find the right financial controller at Indinero to work with but had a terrific experience after I found him
* Quickbooks/Xero + traditional accountant – haven’t explored


Evaluation
* It’s more important to have a good bookkeeper than it is have good “tech” for accounting (since the efficiencies accrue to the accounting firm and not for you)
* Then it’s about the all in price after including things like R&D tax credits and filing taxes (Federal, State, Local, Franchise, etc)


What makes a good bookkeeper / controller (over and above getting the actual accounting right)?
* Knows the right questions to ask (doesn’t has irrelevant questions) and only has to ask them once
* Responsive to requests
* Has the right attitude
* Fast at incorporating feedback
* Great at solving problems proactively for you without having to ask for input every time
* Can handle troubleshooting for you and own the process (doesn’t just refer you to helpdesk)


Next steps
* Since I’m already familiar with Indinero, I’d likely just ask for a demo with Pilot
   * Check out features (most likely similar)
      * What’s onboarding like?
      * What do you have to do month to month to upkeep?
      * What do you have to do each year? Especially for taxes
   * Then ask the sales rep why I should choose pilot over Indinero (and ask Indinero why I should choose them over Pilot)
